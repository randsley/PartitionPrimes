# PartitionPrimes Project Overview

## Current Paper

**Title:** Extending Partition-Theoretic Prime Detection: A Computational Study of the Weight-12 Barrier, the Expression E5, and a Basis-Dependent Recovery of E6

**Author:** Nigel Randsley

**Files:** `paper.tex`, `paper.pdf`

### Background

Craig, van Ittersum, and Ono (CIO2024) introduced non-negative polynomial combinations of MacMahon partition functions M_a(n) that vanish precisely at primes, producing explicit expressions E1, E2, E3, E4. This paper extends that computational framework using exact rational arithmetic.

The MacMahon function U_a(q) is a quasimodular form of weight 2a on SL_2(Z). For a in {1,...,5} (weight <= 10), the relevant quasimodular spaces are spanned entirely by Eisenstein series, so M_a(p) reduces to a polynomial in p at primes. Weight 12 (a=6) is the first weight where cusp forms appear.

### Four Main Results (all computationally bounded, not claimed as general theorems)

1. **Weight-12 barrier** — In the basis {M1,...,M6}, the M6-columns are pivot columns for all tested degrees d in {0,...,6} using 95 primes in [2,500]. No new prime-vanishing direction involving M6 exists within this restricted basis. The obstruction is M6's τ(p) component (Ramanujan's tau-function), which does not reduce to a polynomial in p.

2. **Emergence of E5** — Restricting to {M1,...,M5}, a new primitive prime-vanishing direction E5 first appears at polynomial degree 2. Explicit formula:

   ```
   E5(n) = (-450450 + 675675n - 225225n²) M1(n)
           + (960960n - 120120n²) M2(n)
           + (2534912n - 166016n²) M3(n)
           + (7999488n - 322560n²) M4(n)
           + 258048000 M5(n)
   ```

   Verified: E5(n)=0 for all 95 tested primes in [2,500]; E5(n)≠0 for tested composites.

3. **Basis-dependent recovery via E6** — Enlarging to {M1,...,M7}, a τ-cancellation mechanism yields E6 at degree 4. M6 and M7 together can annihilate both τ(p) and p·τ(p) contributions. This recovery is basis-dependent: it does not occur within {M1,...,M6} alone.

4. **Bounded completeness** — Exhaustive exact search through a_max=12 and d≤8 finds no independent prime-vanishing directions beyond E1,...,E6.

### Key Structural Insight

M6(n) has a closed-form involving Ramanujan's tau-function:

```
M6(n) = sum_{j=0}^{5} P_j(n) σ_{2j+1}(n) - (17 / 150450048000) τ(n)
```

The nonzero coefficient c_τ = -17/150450048000 is the obstruction. Its denominator factors as 2^10 × 3^5 × 5^3 × 7 × 691, where 691 is the Bernoulli prime indexing the weight-12 Eisenstein series (related to τ(n) ≡ σ_{11}(n) mod 691).

## Research Extension Plan (from "Paper review and feedback.docx")

### Recommended Two-Paper Follow-Up Program

#### Paper 1 — The Obstruction Theorem

**Working title:** *The Weight-12 Cusp Obstruction in Partition-Theoretic Prime Detection*

**Goal:** Prove structurally (not just computationally) that the τ(p) cusp contribution from M6 cannot be cancelled within the restricted basis {M1,...,M6}.

**Core theorem target (Theorem A):** In the span of {n^k M_a(n): 0≤k≤d, 1≤a≤6}, the M6-sector contributes a nonzero prime-level cusp component proportional to τ(p), and no new prime-vanishing relation in this restricted basis can cancel that component using only M1,...,M5.

**Key lemmas:**
- Lemma 1: The prime restrictions of M1,...,M5 lie in an Eisenstein-type polynomial/divisor-sum span.
- Lemma 2: The prime restriction of M6 contains a nonzero cusp contribution of the form c·τ(p), c ≠ 0.
- Lemma 3: No nonzero element of the Eisenstein-type prime span generated by M1,...,M5 can agree with a nonzero multiple of τ(p) on all sufficiently many primes. *(This is the crux.)*

**Proof strategy for Lemma 3:** Derive a quasimodular decomposition for U6(q), pass from the coefficient formula for M6(n) to its prime restriction, separate Eisenstein and cusp parts, prove the cusp part cannot be cancelled, deduce the pivot behavior of M6-columns structurally.

#### Paper 2 — The Recovery Theorem

**Working title:** *Cusp Cancellation and the First Recovery of Prime-Vanishing Relations Beyond Weight 12*

**Goal:** Prove that adjoining M7 makes the cusp obstruction cancellable, and the first recovery occurs exactly at degree 4.

**Core theorem target (Theorem B):** In the enlarged basis {n^k M_a(n): 0≤k≤d, 1≤a≤7}, the obstruction contributed by the cusp sector becomes cancellable only after adjoining M7, and the first new recovered prime-vanishing direction occurs at degree 4.

**Stronger structural version (Theorem B'):** At the first recovery stage, the prime-level cusp obstruction space is two-dimensional, represented by τ(p) and p·τ(p), and degree 4 is the minimal polynomial degree at which this obstruction can be annihilated inside the enlarged basis.

**Key lemmas:**
- Lemma 4: The prime restriction of M7 contains cusp contributions spanning the same obstruction sector as τ(p) and p·τ(p).
- Lemma 5: The pair τ(p), p·τ(p) is independent over the Eisenstein-type prime span relevant to the enlarged basis.
- Lemma 6: For d<4, the polynomial freedom is insufficient to annihilate the full obstruction space.
- Lemma 7: At d=4, the enlarged basis admits a nontrivial annihilating combination, yielding the first recovered direction.

### Longer-Term Synthesis

**Provisional title:** *Obstruction Layers and Finite Generation in Partition-Theoretic Prime Detection*

**Main conjectures:**
- Conjecture 1: New prime-vanishing generators arise exactly when new modular obstruction layers become cancellable in enlarged quasimodular bases.
- Conjecture 2: Each obstruction layer contributes a finite-dimensional prime-level cusp space, and the appearance of a new generator is controlled by the first degree at which that space can be annihilated.
- Conjecture 3: The vector space of prime-vanishing relations and the cone of non-negative prime-detecting expressions have distinct structural behavior; finite generation in the former does not immediately determine the extremal structure of the latter.

### Additional Directions

**Computational:** Extend the search beyond a_max=12 and d≤8; track where new cusp dimensions appear; test whether every future nullity jump matches a predicted obstruction-cancellation count.

**Geometric:** Study the cone of non-negative prime-detecting expressions separately from the full prime-vanishing space. E5 is a natural generator but not naturally non-negative (requires correction by E4), suggesting the cone has interesting extremal structure distinct from the vector space.

### Recommended Theorem Sequence (Summary)

1. **Theorem 1:** The prime restriction of M6 contains a nonzero cusp contribution proportional to τ(p) that cannot be cancelled inside the restricted basis {M1,...,M5}.
2. **Corollary 2:** No new prime-vanishing relation in the restricted basis {M1,...,M6} can involve the M6-sector.
3. **Theorem 3:** After adjoining M7, the cusp obstruction becomes cancellable, and the first such cancellation occurs at degree 4, producing the relation corresponding to E6.

### Practical Roadmap

1. Derive a clean modular decomposition for M6 and M7
2. Isolate the prime-evaluation obstruction space
3. Prove non-cancellation in the restricted basis (Paper 1)
4. Prove first cancellation in the enlarged basis (Paper 2)
5. Extend computation to test the next obstruction layer
